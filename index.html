    I’m Luniva Joshi, a Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science student at Rutgers University who loves building, coding, and exploring the intersection between robotics and intelligent systems. My journey in engineering began with a fascination for how machines think and move, and it has since evolved into a drive to design systems that learn, adapt, and inspire. I’ve contributed to proposing a NASA L’SPACE mission, where I collaborated on robotics and systems engineering proposals for future space exploration. Through CodePath, I’ve built Android applications that merge creativity and functionality, while deepening my skills in software design and cybersecurity. On campus, I’m an active member of Rutgers Formula SAE and AIAA’s RU Autonomous team, working on mechanical design, perception, and control for competitive autonomous vehicles. Beyond engineering, I’m passionate about STEM outreach. As a coding instructor, I teach kids how to code, design games, and build robots using platforms like Arduino and LEGO Spike. Seeing students light up when their code comes to life reminds me why I fell in love with engineering in the first place. I recently began research in Rutgers’ robotics labs, where I’m developing skills in robotic system design, focusing on integration between mechanical structures, embedded hardware, and intelligent sensing. My goal is to continue bridging hardware and intelligence to develop robotic systems that push the boundaries of exploration, both on Earth and beyond.
